SVM 01 — What is Space Vector Modulation?

Motor control starts with three-phase electrical quantities produced by an inverter and consumed by a motor. To understand and control them, we represent these quantities geometrically as vectors in simple coordinate systems. From this foundation, techniques like SVM and FOC build higher-level behavior.

Space Vector Modulation (SVM) is the method used in motor-drive inverters to create a desired three-phase voltage by intelligently switching between the inverter’s limited hardware states over time. In practice, this reduces current ripple and torque ripple, and typically improves DC-bus usage compared to simpler modulation.

The core idea (step by step)

A 3-phase inverter used in motor drives can only switch its transistors fully ON or fully OFF. Because of this hardware limitation, it cannot directly generate an arbitrary voltage.

Think of SVM as a simple 3-step ladder:
  1. Hardware: the inverter can only do ON/OFF switching.
  2. Limits: those ON/OFF combinations create only a limited set of voltage possibilities.
  3. Strategy: SVM decides how to switch over time to approximate the voltage we want (we’ll do this later).

In other words, SVM works within the inverter’s constraints by choosing a sequence of valid inverter states defined by the hardware.

The αβ plane (our shared picture)

Instead of thinking in three separate phase waveforms (A, B, C), we use a single 2D picture that is easier to reason about.

The αβ plane is simply a 2D way to represent three-phase electrical quantities as a single rotating vector, making them easier to visualize and reason about.

You can also think of it this way: the αβ plane is a 2D coordinate system that represents three-phase electrical quantities as a single vector, providing a clean geometric view of their behavior.

If you have seen the Clarke transformation before, the αβ plane will look familiar. If not, don’t worry — you do not need to derive or calculate anything in the αβ plane yet. We are only using it as a visualization tool.

Geometric picture (SVM hexagon)

Before going into timing, PWM, or equations, let’s look only at the geometry.
